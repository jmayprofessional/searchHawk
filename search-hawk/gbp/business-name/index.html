Business Name

Your business name is the foundation of your local SEO. It must be exactly the same everywhere — on Google, your website, and all online directories — to build trust and authority with Google.

Why this matters

Google uses your business name to match your profile to search queries. If it varies across platforms, Google may not trust your business, which can hurt rankings.

Example: Your registered business name is “Smith Plumbing LLC”. Do not use “Smith Plumbing Charleston” or “Smith Plumbing Services.”

Checklist

  • Business name matches your legal / registered name exactly (check your incorporation documents or license)
  • Do not add keywords, locations, or extra words — e.g., avoid “Best Plumbing Charleston”
  • Name matches your website homepage exactly, including punctuation and abbreviations
  • Name matches major local directories (Yelp, BBB, Angi, etc.)

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using different abbreviations: “LLC” vs “L.L.C.”
  • Adding extra words like “best,” “cheap,” or “services”
  • Changing the order of words: e.g., “Plumbing Smith LLC” instead of “Smith Plumbing LLC”
  • Not updating citations after a name change
