Google Search Console

Use Google Search Console (GSC) to monitor your website’s performance in local search results. This guide will help you understand what to track, how to use it, and how often to check your metrics.

What is Google Search Console?

Google Search Console is a free tool from Google that shows how your website is performing in search results. You can see which keywords people are using to find your business, which pages rank best, and identify technical issues that might prevent your site from ranking.

Why you should use it

  • Track which search terms bring visitors to your site.
  • Identify pages that are underperforming so you can optimize them.
  • Monitor your website for indexing errors or security issues.
  • See how Google views your website, including mobile usability and page speed.

How to get started

  1. Go to Google Search Console and log in with your Google account.
  2. Add your website by verifying ownership using one of the recommended methods (HTML file upload, meta tag, Google Analytics, or Google Tag Manager).
  3. Submit your sitemap (usually located at yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml) to help Google index your pages.
  4. Allow a few days for Google to collect data on your website’s performance.

Key metrics to monitor

  • Total Clicks: How many people clicked on your website from Google Search.
  • Total Impressions: How many times your pages appeared in search results.
  • Average CTR: Percentage of impressions that resulted in clicks.
  • Average Position: Your page’s average ranking for the queries it appears for.
  • Coverage Issues: Errors or warnings that prevent pages from being indexed.
  • Mobile Usability: Check if your site works well on mobile devices.

Using GSC to improve your local business

Once you start getting data, focus on the pages and keywords that bring the most traffic. Identify opportunities where you appear on page 2 or 3 and optimize those pages to move up. Check coverage issues weekly and fix them promptly. For local businesses, pay attention to location-based queries and click trends to understand which services or locations drive the most interest.

Monitor your site every 1–2 weeks to ensure your pages stay visible and fix new errors immediately. Over time, this will help your website rank higher, attract more local visitors, and generate more leads.
