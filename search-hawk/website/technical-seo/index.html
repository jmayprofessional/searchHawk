Technical SEO for Local Rankings

Technical SEO ensures Google can crawl, understand, and trust your website so it ranks for local searches.

Below are the key technical steps, why they matter, and examples so you can implement them yourself.

Mobile-Friendly & Fast Loading

Google prioritizes mobile-friendly, fast websites. Slow or broken mobile pages can hurt rankings.

How to do it: Use responsive design, compress images, enable caching, and minimize heavy scripts.

Example: Test using Google PageSpeed Insights and aim for 80+ mobile score.

SSL Certificate (HTTPS)

HTTPS secures your site, protects visitors, and is a ranking factor.

How to do it: Install an SSL certificate (most hosting platforms offer free options like Let’s Encrypt).

Example: Your site should load as https://yourdomain.com, not http://.

Structured Data (Schema)

Adding LocalBusiness schema helps Google understand your business details and display rich results.

How to do it: Include name, address, phone, hours, service area, and services in JSON-LD format.

Example: Google Rich Results Test tool can validate your schema.

Meta Titles & Descriptions

Titles and descriptions tell Google what your page is about and encourage clicks.

How to do it: Include main service + city in title and description naturally.

Example: Title: “Plumber in Charleston, SC | Fast & Reliable”. Description: “Expert plumbing services in Charleston. Call today!”

URL Structure

Clean URLs improve usability and local SEO signals.

How to do it: Use lowercase, dashes for spaces, include service + city.

Example: /plumber-charleston or /drain-cleaning-charleston

Internal Linking

Links help Google understand page relationships and hierarchy.

How to do it: Link homepage → service pages → related services.

Example: On homepage: “Our Services → Emergency Plumbing, Drain Cleaning”

Sitemap & Robots.txt

These files guide search engines to index the right pages.

How to do it: Create sitemap.xml with all pages, and robots.txt allowing search engines to crawl them. Submit sitemap in Google Search Console.
