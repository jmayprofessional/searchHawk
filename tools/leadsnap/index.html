Leadsnap Step-by-Step Guide

Leadsnap is a powerful tool to track your local map pack rankings, create heatmaps, and monitor competitors. This guide walks you through the process for your business.

By the end, you’ll know exactly where you rank for popular local keywords, which areas to target, and how to monitor progress consistently.

Step 1: Set Up Your Account

Sign up for Leadsnap and connect your business. Enter the name, address, phone number (NAP), and all the service locations you serve. Accuracy here is critical for correct local ranking tracking.

  • Verify your account via email
  • Set your primary business category (match your Google Business Profile)
  • Add any secondary categories if relevant

Step 2: Create Your Heatmap

Heatmaps visualize where your business appears in the Google Map Pack for specific local search terms. This is critical for identifying “hot spots” and gaps in visibility.

  • Select your primary service keywords (e.g., “plumber near me”, “emergency plumbing Charleston”)
  • Set the geographic area to your service cities
  • Generate the heatmap to see your visibility across neighborhoods

Example: If your business appears only in the downtown area but not in surrounding suburbs, you know where to focus marketing and SEO efforts.

Step 3: Competitor Analysis

Identify who’s outranking you and for which keywords. Leadsnap will show their rankings and map visibility so you can reverse-engineer their strategy.

  • Focus on top competitors in your service areas
  • Look for patterns in the keywords they rank for
  • Note any business types, reviews, or backlink signals that may give them an edge

Tip: Keep a spreadsheet of competitor insights for quick reference when updating your GBP or website.

Step 4: Monitor Your Rankings

Consistency is key. Check your rankings weekly or monthly to see improvements or drops, and correlate them with changes you make to your GBP, website, or citations.

  • Schedule weekly or bi-weekly reports in Leadsnap
  • Compare heatmap changes over time
  • Identify what’s working (new citations, reviews, posts) and what needs adjustment

Example: A keyword like “emergency plumbing Charleston” moves from page 2 to page 1 in the map pack — you know your strategy is working!

Step 5: Take Action

Use the insights from Leadsnap to improve your local SEO:

  • Add new service area pages to your website
  • Post updates and offers to your Google Business Profile
  • Collect more reviews and respond quickly
  • Build local backlinks and citations in underrepresented areas
